Our story

Built with purpose

FateDate began with a simple observation about modern dating.

In 2017, someone from our local community tragically lost their life after meeting someone through a dating app in a private setting. It was heartbreaking and it raised important questions about how people meet today.

As online dating evolved, most platforms focused on conversation, but not what comes after. People could connect easily, but moving from connection to a real-world meeting often felt uncertain, unstructured, or left entirely to chance.

At the same time, serious daters were navigating environments where genuine intent, casual use, misrepresentation and bad actors could all exist in the same space, making the experience less clear and less intentional than it should be.

It became clear that how people meet shouldn’t be left entirely to chance. It should be structured, considered, and designed around real-world interaction.

We measure success differently. Not by time spent in an app, but by real moments — when two people meet, feel genuine chemistry, and choose to continue.

“Finally an app that actually puts safety and intent first.”

Our mission

To make dating safer and more intentional, helping people connect with the right person and build relationships that last.

Safety first

Every member is ID-verified before connecting, helping create a more trusted environment from the start.

Designed for real connection

Every feature is designed to reduce friction, minimise risk, and support real in-person meetings where genuine chemistry can be experienced.

Real people, real places

First dates take place in partnered venues designed as flexible environments for real-world connection.

Privacy, always

Your main profile stays hidden from non-connections. A public profile helps you connect, and you control what is shared after connecting.

Clarity over noise

People connect with clear intent, reducing uncertainty and helping everyone understand who they are meeting and why.

Built to last

This isn’t about short-term attention. It’s about supporting connections that can grow beyond the first meeting.
